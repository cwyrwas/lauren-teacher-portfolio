My first philosophy

What is progressivism?

Progressivism is the idea that students should be active in the classroom. My job as a teacher is to view students as individuals with their own experiences and thought processes. The student's jobs are giving input into the things they want to learn, the goals they want to meet, and the rules of the classroom. This connects to progressivism by allowing students to be independent thinkers and experimenters who have a say in their own learning!

My second philosophy

What is Social Reconstructionism?

Social reconstructionism is a belief that encourages students to examine and solve real-world problems in society. My job as a teacher is to teach students about these problems. The students' job is to come up with solutions to these problems. The end goal for this philosophy is to create change both in and out of the classroom.

Professional Development

How I will keep learning

After I graduate college, I will still be learning every day, especially in the classroom. In my classroom, I plan to learn by always asking my students for feedback on what they liked and what they did not like. I will try to use this feedback to change future lessons. I always want to work with students, not against them!

I also want to practice personal reflection. Every time I finish a lesson or assessment, I want to practice looking back at the lesson I taught. While reflecting, I want to think about what I liked and what I did not like about the lesson. I want to implement what I liked in my future lesson and avoid what I did not like.

How I will make it inviting

My classroom environment

My number one goal for my classroom is to make it welcoming for all students from all backgrounds. This is how I will do that:

  • Give all my students fair and equal opportunities to work
  • Reminding students that they are all valuable and worthy
  • Reminding students that there are no bad ideas
  • Teaching students to honor all identities
  • Encourage students to work together for the benefit of the classroom
  • Teaching students that change is good
“Children...need most of the same things adults need--consideration, respect for their work, the knowledge that they and the things they do are taken seriously.” ― Caroline Pratt

Setting goals

How my classroom will work

Students will be tasked with creating the classroom they want to learn in. Students will be allowed to set their own rules and goals. Additionally, students will be given some opportunities to work with the teacher to create objectives for what they want to learn. This will be done with careful monitoring and guidance from the teacher. By allowing students to create their own environment, they will be more likely to hold each other accountable to follow the rules!

Communication in and out of the classroom

Parent and Guardian Communication

I will implement communication folders! This is a simple, yet effective way for teachers and guardians of students to communicate. At the end of every week, I will send home a folder updating guardians on what students did that week, upcoming events, and student behavior for the week. Parents and Guardians are encouraged to write back with any questions, comments or concerns!

Additionally, I would love to implement regular conferences between parents and guardians. The frequency of these conferences will be dependent on students and their guardians, and these conferences can be virtual or in-person. I would also love to give calls home when a student does something exceptional. This could be the student getting a good grade on something they struggled with or doing something kind for a friend!

How I will implement diversity into the classroom

Diversity in the classroom

I want my classroom to be a safe space for every single student no matter their background, cultural identity, or socioeconomic status. I will do this by:

  • Celebrating cultures from around the world
  • Encouraging students to share about their cultures
  • Modifying assignments for students who have special needs
  • Working with different supports for students who have special needs
  • Having food in my classroom for student who may need it
  • Encouraging all students to be kind to themselves and their classmates
  • Celebrating differences
  • Working with parents and guardians when a student is struggling
  • Teaching lessons about other cultures

Conclusion

My ultimate goals for the classroom

Ultimately, I want my classroom to be a space where all students can create, thrive, experiment, question, and be independent. I hope to achieve this by using my teaching philosophies. Additionally, I want to invite parent and guardian collaboration and communication into the classroom, so that all parties involved can create meaningful experiences for students in the classroom!

I also want my classroom to be full of creativity. I would love to include students' artwork as decorations for all around the classroom. I also want to encourage creativity in many of the lessons and assignments by giving students the option to do something creative for homework. By doing this, students will be able to grow their creative thinking skills and independence. These ideas all stem from progressivism and social reconstructionism.
